Know me

Gabor Monspart retired cavalry colonel, horse riding trainer

He was born at Zalaegerszeg on the 13th of April 1906. He graduated in his home town and in that year he succesfully applied the four year course at the Ludovika Academy, which he finished in 1928.
He was an active sportsman already as a Ludovika student. In April 1927 he took part to the first Hungarian pentathlon race. He did pentathlon later too and he was cap the pentathlon.
After graduating the Academy he first served at Kecskemet than at Nyiregyhaza and he also fulfilled equitation here in 1930. In 1932 he was placed to Orkeny camp, to the riding trainer school where he attended a two year course. After graduating as trainer he was set to the 3rd Nadasdy cavalry. At this time he regulary took part of national jumping, compound and military horse races.
After the get back of Felvidek at Munkacs and after the transilvanian entry he served at Nagyvarad. From July 1 until September 15 he was at the Russian front line with the legion. The almost catastrophical injuries caused by the saddles of the legion horses attracted his attention to the troubles of the Hungarian military (35 M.) Bock saddles and based on the experiences gained on the front he made a corrected version of saddle, that was known as 42 M. saddle at the national defense (Monspart style corrected saddle).
In 1943 he was promoted major. In 1944 was in Poland, near Bug (the Pripety swamp) taking part of horse division, beginning the second part of October he took part of the battles between the rivers Danube and Tisza, in Hungary, than in the beginning of November they stationed on the Csepel island. On the 5th of November he was wounded during a plane attack at Szaszhalombatta. After a short hospital treatment he returned to his division, but he didn’t want to take part anymore of the senseless battles and while his division left the country on the 31st of March 1945, he remained in Hungary refering to his wound.
On the 10th of April 1945 at Miskolc he checked in the newly formed national defense. On the 30th of April 1945 he was promoted lieutenant-colonel. He served in the new army until the 6th of September 1946, when he was set reservist. After that he found a job in Komarom at the Horse Salesmanship Association than in 1947 he took an exam of horsebreeding and he worked as breeding chief supervisor at Komarom stud.
On the 29th of August 1950 he was arrested together with his former division commander, Dezso Nemeth. On the 13th of September 1951 based upon false accusation, because of war guilts he was sentenced of eight years prison and degradation. He was imprisoned at Marianostra.
On the 25th of June 1954 he asked for his court case retaken. Though the court still found him guilty, but reducing his punishment he was sentenced to the time already spent in jail and he was set free on the 17th of December 1954. When he was imprisoned and during the court cases Gabor Monspart declared himself not guilty, that was accepted at his rehabilitation in 1991. He got back his grade and he was promoted colonel.
After he was set free he started to work again at the Komarom stud, he became the manager of the artificial breeding station in Devecser and he took part of the equine sport life on the stud. He contributed to the organization of the regional races, to the work of the jury.
In 1959 he publicated in the journal called Pegazus. In a little while – to the reccomendation of the National Horse Breeding Assosiation - he became employed by The Touristic Office, the manager of the Nagyvázsony riding school that was established in 1962. Although the riding school was opened for touristic purpose, there was opportunity for sport activities too. The attention of Gabor Monspart was focused first of all to military, that by that time in Hungary was neglected, almost didn’t exist. Gabor Monspart had a big role in resurrection of this sport branch also being helped by the enthusiasm of some sport associations accross the Danube. In a short time the riders and horses from Nagyvazsony got in the front line. He also took part in preparing for the München Olimpic Games, but the sport leaders, ignoring him, used only the horses trained by him.
In 1974 Gabor Monspart was asked to teach at the Kaposvar Breeding High School Horse Academy. He worked there until his retirement. His favourite sport remained military. He was the leader of the military team that was training for the Moscow Olimpic Games in 1980. As a retired riding teacher he trained many young riders in Kaposvar and he wrote several functional and articles, high school notes. He mentioned in one of his articles that in prison he asked for books to read and he got a book on the Pavlov conditioned responses. Thanks to these readings later he dealt a lot with the role of the conditioned responses in training horses. He had many articles regarding to this in the journal called the „Horse breeding and horse sport”. He valued the curiosity and endeavour of the enquirers. He tried to give approach: "Learn to make difference between correct and incorrect, that you only learn what is correct because people often learn quicker what is incorrect than correct things!" He worked with the thoroughbreads which ended their career as trotters, later on he dealt with gidrans in groups of five-six. In the pair of horse and rider always the horse was the first for him, as he often mentioned:

"I work with the horse and with it’s engine: the hindquater!"
He worked according to the classical training and never diverged of the basic principle of his ccolleague, Agoston Endrody – Give your horse a chance! During his trainings he asked with a great importance the cadence, the relaxation, than as a result of the previously mentioned the proper in all three strides. The horsemen working with him had to be able to ride their horses in the three strides and in different field conditions only by holding the buckle, without using the reins to control horses. To this took part the jumping too, that like Mr. Nemethy he had had it done in trot. He improved the harmony between horse and rider with tests like asking the roder to get his horse through ash lines that doesn’t mean barrier for the horse (to get there like to a pole put on the ground). Through these it is clear that his methods were not different from those of the nowadays fashionable and well known horsemen, whose methods are based on the natural behaviour of horses with each other, like for example Buck Brannaman or Pat Parelli, whose work was very much appreciated by famous students of Bertalan Nemethy, such as George Morris and Joe Fargis.
He attributed very much importance to the endowment and natural motivation of horses. He lived for the future. He always tried to answer the questions unselfishly. He was calm and measured. He always worked for the mutual success of the horse and rider.
For his work in the horse sport the Hungarian Horse Association gave him the Szechenyi memorial medal.

Gabor Monspart dealt with horse sport until the end of his long life. He died on the 14th of July 1997. He was buried on the 24th of July 1997 at Kaposvar-Toponar.
