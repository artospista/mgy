About natural horsemanship...

All started in 1989 at the University Riding School in Kaposvar (today Horse Academy).

We, (Gyula and I) first heard – at least compared to the previously experienced – very "differenttly" about horses, about horse riding, about the relationship between horse and human from Gabor Monspart (1906 – 1997), a great horseman who graduated at Orkeny camp.

He talked magnanimously about everything, that he experienced and learned about horses during his long and adventurous career. He did his best to learn us his huge knowledge the most complete way and as a uniform system.

Naturally in the middle of this system is the horse. The starting point of his trainings and his theoretically reasoning were always the natural attributions of horses, their natural reactions to the enviromental changes. He always searched the answers to the problems of the practical life using his own experiences and scientific methods, knowledges (genetical, biomechanical, neurological etc.). We had listened to him with amazement and we had done our best to understand, to learn as much as we could about this "miracle". We were sorry for every minute that we spent without him and without studying his methods.

But life overwrited this almost idyllic scene and school had ended before we could learn everything. We had distanced (only phisically, in our souls we have been always mates). Our relationship with uncle Gabor had remained but its intensity decreased very much. And we hadn’t been able to answer every question. We had had many unanswered questions. The wiev wasn’t complete, important details had been missing.

In my life had appeared other things too than horses and I couldn’t solely deal with horses.

But Gyula have shown big persistency remaining near horses. He met all kind of people, known many techniques and places. Continuously augmenting his knowledge and experiences tried to answer the previously mentioned questions. During his life path searching a couple of years ago he had shown a display, where Pat Parelli had shown his horse training ( rather "people training") method. And there was a miracle, he realized that this what what he had been searching for, the missing link got to its place. Parelli had lead him to the path where natural horsemanship had gave an answer to the previously unanswered questions. As much he had got involved in studying this method he realised that natural horsemanship is the same as classical riding. With the help of that had became complete the riding technique we have had inherited.

Natural horsemanship is a link between the old times great masters, the horse trainers with good sensation, the cowboys searching for delicate riding and between all horsemen that think and there is very important to them to properly communicate with their horses.
